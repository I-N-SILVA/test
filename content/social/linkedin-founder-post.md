I one-shot a full World Cup game with Fable. Here's the part that should make every founder uncomfortable.

Not a landing page. Not a clickable mockup. A real, playable browser game:

→ 54 nations, 500+ World Cup players from 1930 to 2026
→ a spin-the-wheel draft with four selectable modes
→ a match-simulation engine that actually models attack vs defence
→ daily challenges, seeded runs, shareable result cards

The premise is simple and brutal: spin the wheel, draft an all-time XI, and try to win the entire 2026 World Cup — eight matches, zero goals conceded. We call it the Perfect Run.

I described what I wanted. Fable built it. The first working version came out of essentially a single prompt.

The lesson isn't "AI writes code now." We've known that for a while.

The lesson is about the gap between an idea and something real. That gap used to be measured in sprints and budgets and "let's circle back next quarter." For this, it was measured in a single sitting. The cost of testing an idea has collapsed — which means the only real bottleneck left is having the idea and the taste to know if it's any good.

If you're sitting on a "someday" project, the excuse just got a lot weaker.
