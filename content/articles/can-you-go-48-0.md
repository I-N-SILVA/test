# Can You Go 48-0?

The 2026 World Cup is the biggest in history. **48 teams. Three host nations. 104 matches.** And somewhere in the noise of group-stage chaos and penalty heartbreak, a simple, evil little question started following me around:

Not *can you win the World Cup* — but **can you win it perfectly?**

Eight matches. Eight wins. Zero goals conceded. That's the **Perfect Run**, and it's the whole reason this game exists. Most people never do it. That's the point.

## The pitch in one breath

**Perfect Run** is a browser game. You spin a wheel, it lands on a real World Cup nation, and you draft one legend from that country into your XI. Pelé or Ronaldo? Maldini or Cafu? Beckenbauer or Bobby Moore? You keep spinning until all eleven shirts are filled — then you simulate a full 2026 run: three group games, then Round of 32, 16, quarters, semis, and the Final.

Win them all without conceding, and you've joined a club that, statistically, almost nobody is in.

It's the international cousin of those *38-0* and *82-0* perfect-season games — but built entirely around the tournament that only comes around once every four years.

## Why it's quietly addictive

- **510+ player-seasons across 54 nations**, spanning 1930 to 2026. A 2002 Ronaldo and a 1998 Ronaldo are *different cards*, because they were different players.
- **Every spin is a gamble.** Open draw gives all 54 nations equal odds. Land on Brazil and you're spoiled. Land on Curaçao and you're improvising. That tension is the game.
- **Chemistry rewards bravery.** Stack players from one nation or one era and you get a bonus — but you'll have to pass up better players to do it. Risk versus reward, every pick.
- **It actually simulates football.** Your forwards and midfield decide the goals you score. Your defenders and goalkeeper decide the clean sheets. A back five plays nothing like a front three — and the Perfect Run lives and dies on your defence.
- **Daily Challenge.** Everyone gets the same seed each day — the same wheel, the same opponents. So when your mate says they got to the semis, you can run the *exact same draft* and prove you'd have done better.

## How to play in 30 seconds

1. **Pick your shape** — 4-3-3, 3-5-2, a back five, whatever you can defend with.
2. **Pick your spin mode** — Open draw, Realistic (weighted by World Cup appearances), Confederation (spin a continent, then a nation), or Position-first.
3. **Spin. Draft. Repeat** until your XI is full. Feeling brave? Burn a 🎲 **gamble token** and let the game draft a *completely random* player for you. Could be prime Zidane. Could be a 73-rated debutant in goal.
4. **Simulate all 8 matches** and watch the scorelines roll in.
5. **Share your run card** and dare someone to beat it.

## FAQ

**Is it free?**
Completely. No account, no download, no ads. Your run saves in your browser, so you can close the tab and come back.

**Where do the ratings come from?**
They're community-curated, per player per World Cup year. You'll disagree with some of them. That's half the fun — corrections are welcome on GitHub.

**Is this an official FIFA game?**
No. Perfect Run is an independent, fan-made project. It's not affiliated with, endorsed by or associated with FIFA, any federation or any ratings provider. No official logos, crests or player images are used.

**How rare is an actual 48-0… sorry, 8-0 Perfect Run?**
Rare enough that we named the whole game after it. Win every match *and* concede nothing. Most runs die in the knockouts. Yours might too.
